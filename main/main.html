COLOR ANALYSIS IN 19TH CENTURY LITERATURE

ABOUT

This project approaches literature from a computational standpoint. We apply natural language processing, statistics, and data visualization techniques to the analysis of 19th century literature. Our main research question is whether the use of complex colors, like ochre, are found in more complex contexts, and whether, analogously, simple colors like red are found in more simple contexts. Of course, complexity can mean many things, so we've collected 100+ features of sentences (e.g. vocabulary richness, periodicity, average word length, etc.) that are all facets of complexity. There are also simpler trends that we are looking at â€“ such as whether mentions of colors with the base color red (e.g. crimson, scarlet, etc.) increased over the course of the 19th century, and whether the parts-of-speech of colors changed over time (e.g. are they more frequently present as adjectives than nouns).
